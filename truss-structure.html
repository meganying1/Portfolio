Truss Structure

Manufacturing and assembling a truss structure designed to fail under a 40 pound load.

Abstract

In this project, I worked with a team to design a truss. Our goal was to apply structural analysis to create a truss that would break under a 40-pound load, while also keeping its weight at a minimum. We generated parts and assemblies using SolidWorks and used a laser cutting machine to create our members.

Design Process

My team began the design process by sketching 9 potential structures that satisfied the truss design rules. We then calculated the force applied to each member in a truss design when a 40-pound weight was hung from the center. This value was used to determine the strength-to-weight ratio of each truss. From there, we used truss design with the highest ratio as our final design. Once we chose our final design, we applied stress analysis and failure safety to calculate the width of the members required to break at 40 pounds. My team then used CAD to create a model of our design.

Once we decided the exact dimensions required for the members of our truss, we created DXF files of each member we needed to build our truss. We imported these files to CorelDRAW software, adjusted our settings to cut through acrylic, and ran the file. After the laser cutter successfully cut out all our needed members, we assembled the members using screws and bolts to create our prototype.

On testing day, our truss failed at approximately 38 pounds. Through this project, I was able to apply basic mechanical principles including 2D equilibrium, stress analysis, and failure theory to a real-world application. I also learned about the role of using CAD and laser machines in prototyping.
