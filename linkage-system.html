Linkage System

Designing and assembling a linkage system capable of converting rotary motion of a motor into linear motion of pressing a button.

Abstract

I worked with a group to design a linkage system capable of converting rotary motion of a motor into linear motion of pressing a button. The goal of this project was to optimize the amount of time the button is pressed, while also considering geometry, materials, loading, and workspace constraints.

Design and Analysis Process

My team began brainstorming designs by drawing inspiration from the crank rocker mechanism. We varied the mounting position of the assembly and the lengths of the links, as well as experimented with including slotted portions. We decided to create a design with a slotted link, because we believed it would allow the system to be held in a vertical position for a longer period of time. Afterwards, my group members and I used Solidworks to generate models and run motion simulations of our designs, allowing us to select the one that would lead to the highest button pressing time. Finally, we ran an FEA simulation and used hand calculations to dimension our links.

Iteration Process

On the first testing day, our assembly was able to press the button without failing or causing the motor to stall. However, the slot did not work, causing the system to descend sooner and more rapidly than expected. Consequently, the button was only pressed for a total 3 seconds during a period of 2 minutes. We then improved the design using our knowledge of Grashofâ€™s criterion and observations from testing. We lengthened two of our links and relocated the mounting position of our assembly. We did not believe the slot would function due to friction but kept it in our design to minimize the weight of the system. We then repeated the analysis process to ensure no failure would occur.

Results

During the final round of testing, the system descended slowly and smoothly with no abrupt drops. As a result, our assembly pressed the button for more than 41 seconds during the 2-minute period, increasing the total button press time by 38 seconds. This project demonstrated the importance of iteration for continuous importance in the design process. By completing the project, I learned how to use Solidworks motion simulations to visualize the behavior of assemblies. I was also able to practice applying what I have learned about stress analysis to an actual product.
